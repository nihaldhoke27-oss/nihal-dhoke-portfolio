Research Insights

Top Findings from the LinkedIn Research

10 evidence-based insights from analyzing the world's top B2B LinkedIn creators

01

Founder Content = 8–10× Company Page

Data from Refine Labs: founder LinkedIn posts receive 8–10× more engagement per impression than equivalent company page posts.

Source: Chris Walker / Refine Labs
02

Dark Social Hides 40–60% of Pipeline

LinkedIn-influenced pipeline shows up as "Direct" in CRM. Self-reported attribution reveals the true source of 40–60% of pipeline.

Source: Refine Labs Research
03

The 90-Day Compound Curve

Consistent posting for 90 days builds algorithmic trust. Most creators quit on day 73 — right before the algorithm starts paying compound interest.

Source: Justin Welsh, Lara Acosta
04

Specificity is a Growth Multiplier

"I help marketers" attracts nobody. "I help B2B SaaS CMOs generate pipeline from LinkedIn" attracts exactly the right people at scale.

Source: Lara Acosta
05

Comments Outweigh Likes 10:1

LinkedIn's algorithm weights comments dramatically higher than likes. Design posts to invite debate, not just approval.

Source: LinkedIn Algorithm Research
06

Never Put Links in Post Body

External links in post body reduce organic reach by 50–80%. Always put links in the first comment and reference it in the post.

Source: Justin Welsh, Algorithm Docs
07

Write Your Hook Last

The best hooks are distillations of the completed post — written last, not first. Spend 50% of editing time on the first sentence.

Source: Jasmin Alić
08

Demand Gen ≠ Lead Gen

LinkedIn organic creates demand in the 95% not yet in market. Most B2B teams only do lead gen — capturing demand they never created.

Source: Chris Walker / Refine Labs
09

Create Once, Distribute Forever

One piece of content distributed across 12 channels over 12 months generates 10× the ROI of content published once and forgotten.

Source: Ross Simmonds / Foundation
10

LinkedIn Seeds Every Other Channel

In every case study: LinkedIn organic content was the foundational channel that seeded newsletters, communities, and revenue. Build it first.

Source: Multiple case studies
